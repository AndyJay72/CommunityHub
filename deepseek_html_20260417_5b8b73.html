OUR STORY

About the Hall

Opened in 1983 after 12 years of dedication by local volunteers, Grimsargh Village Hall has been the beating heart of our community for over four decades.

In 2010 we completed a major extension and development, followed by a 10kWp solar array in 2012 which significantly reduces our carbon footprint. The facilities were further upgraded in 2017 with a full refit of all toilets and accessible amenities.

Managed by a registered charity, the hall provides a vibrant space for cultural, educational, and recreational activities — from Toddlers Playgroup to the Women's Institute, Badminton Club, Gardening Club, and Parish Council meetings.

Monthly tradition since 2006

Grimsargh Farmers' Market

Running the third Saturday of every month (except January & August), our market brings 20–30 stalls of local Lancashire produce, crafts, and delicious street food.

Fresh meats & cheeses
Artisan bread & pies
Local gin & fudge
Handmade crafts & gifts

Alongside the market, our pop-up café serves breakfast barms, toasties, cakes, and fresh coffee — all run by volunteers, with proceeds supporting the hall.
